TikTok Privacy and Policy

What data is collected from the user?

Cookies

Upon logging into Tiktok, users will be asked whether or not to accept cookies. According to Tiktok’s privacy policy, cookies are small text files that enable Tiktok and service providers to uniquely identify users' browsers and devices. It also assigns a unique number to the user's device and stores the information recorded by the websites. Cookies collect data, measure, and analyze users' website and platform visits, or access to improve customer service and technical support, and to provide relevant advertising on the platform across devices.

Cookies come in two types, they can either be a session cookie or a persistent cookie. Session cookies are active while the browser is open but deleted automatically once the user closes the browser. It cannot be used for tracking browsers and devices. On the other hand, persistent cookies are retained even after the user closes the browser. Additionally, persistent cookies have expiration dates, which could be after a few days, weeks or months. If the user returns to the website, then the website or service provider can recognize the persistent cookies and recognize your browsing devices.

Tiktok also utilizes other technologies such as SDKs, pixel tags, and local storage to store information

  • Software Development Kits (SDKs) are pieces of code that collect information about a user's device, network, and interaction with a website. It also assigns a unique number to users.
  • Pixels (also called beacons or tags) are small 1 pixel x 1 pixel invisible image files embedded in certain website pages or emails. It can be used to collect information about users' websites or record email access.
  • Local storage stores files created by websites on the device speed up site functionality, features, and remember your preferences or interests

Essentially, SDKs, pixels, and local storage are technologies that store information, enable certain features and functionality, and distinguish a user from other visitors.

Furthermore, Tiktok utilizes an Application Programmable Interface or API, which is a piece of software that facilitates communication between Tiktok, applications, and service providers

Data collected by cookies

Data is collected and processed whether a user created an account or not.Tiktok collects personal information when a user creates an account and uploads content to the platform. When a user downloads content and interacts with the platform,Tiktok still collects data about the user without creating an account. The type of data collected are profile information, user content and behavioral information, information from third parties, technical information, location, in-app purchases, and proof of your identity or age.

  • Profile information filled in during the registration refers to username, password, date of birth, email address, telephone number, profile description, and photos or videos
  • User content refers to the content user creates such as photos, videos, comments, and livestreams.
  • Behavioral information refers to preferences and interests of the user considering gender and age. Tiktok also processed information about users' followers, likes and responses to the user content.
  • Information from third parties refers to information you shared from social media platforms, advertisers, and analytics.
  • Technical information refers to information about the device users use, IP address, mobile carrier, time zone, network type, operating system, etcetera.
  • Location refers to approximate or precise location based on SIM, IP Address, and GPS.
  • In-app purchases refers to purchases from Apple itunes or Google play store
  • Proof of users identity or age is a requirement for livestream or verified accounts.

What happens to collected data?

According to Tiktok's Privacy policy, they use the data they've collected to personalize the content users receive and provide them with tailored content that will suit their interest. The collected data are also used for platform troubleshooting, sales, marketing and promoting campaigns. Tiktok claims that they use the user's personal data to improve app suitability while also adhering to government laws. They mentioned that aside from using the data for enhancing app features, they give the information to third party companies in compliance to the contract Tiktok has with their business partners, payment providers, advertisers, merger and law enforcement.

Giving of data to third party
Tiktok provides information and content to service providers who support their business, such as cloud service providers and providers of content moderation services to ensure that the Platform is a safe and enjoyable place and service providers that assist them in marketing the Platform. Tiktok shares users device ID with measurement companies so that they can link user activity on the Platform with your activity on other websites; Tiktok then uses this information to show users adverts which may be of interest to them. In such transactions, user information may be among the transferred assets.
